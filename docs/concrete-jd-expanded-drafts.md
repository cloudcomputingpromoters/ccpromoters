## 1. Junior Concrete Inspector — Denver, CO  `junior-concrete-inspector-denver`
*(APPROVED SAMPLE — focus: pre-placement & field testing · $55,000–$68,000)*

Our Denver client, a respected materials-testing and special-inspection firm, is hiring a Junior Concrete Inspector to support commercial foundation and structural slab work across the Front Range. Denver's construction market is booming — from downtown high-rises and mixed-use developments to suburban distribution centers and transit expansion — and reliable concrete inspection has never been more in demand. In this entry-level role you'll spend your days on active job sites, running fresh-concrete tests and producing the documentation that the engineer of record relies on to accept the work. It's a hands-on opportunity to build the foundation of a long career in construction quality assurance.

## About the Role
A typical day starts early at the batch plant or job site, where you'll review the day's pour schedule, calibrate your test equipment, and meet with the placement crew and project superintendent before concrete arrives. As trucks roll in, you'll sample fresh concrete, run slump, air, and temperature tests, cast cylinders, and watch placement and consolidation to confirm the work matches the approved drawings and specifications. You'll work alongside contractors, project engineers, and senior inspectors, and you'll often be the firm's eyes on the ground — the person whose field notes become the official record of what happened on site that day.

Success at six months looks like this: you're running routine pours independently, your daily reports are clear and submitted on time, contractors trust your calls because you're consistent and fair, and your senior inspector is comfortable assigning you to higher-profile projects. You'll know the Front Range job sites, the local inspectors and labs, and the common mix designs well enough to spot a problem before it becomes a non-conformance.

## What You'll Do
- Conduct pre-placement inspections of formwork, base preparation, and reinforcing steel before each pour
- Perform slump, air content, unit weight, and concrete temperature tests in accordance with ASTM C143, C231, C138, and C1064
- Cast, label, and field-cure compressive-strength cylinders and transport them to the laboratory for break testing
- Verify reinforcing steel size, grade, spacing, and cover against approved structural drawings
- Observe concrete placement, consolidation, and finishing to confirm conformance with project specifications
- Monitor curing and protection methods through the specified curing period
- Write detailed daily field reports documenting placement conditions, quantities, test results, and any non-conformances
- Communicate test results and deficiencies to the project team and your supervising engineer in real time
- Maintain and calibrate field testing equipment and keep accurate calibration records

## Required Qualifications
- High school diploma or GED
- 0–2 years of experience in concrete inspection, materials testing, or construction quality control
- Valid driver's license and reliable transportation to Front Range job sites
- Ability to read and interpret construction documents, structural drawings, and specifications
- Strong written communication skills for clear, defensible daily reporting
- Comfortable working outdoors in variable Colorado weather and on active construction sites
- ACI Concrete Field Testing Technician — Grade I (mandatory)

## Preferred Qualifications

- Associate degree or coursework in construction management, civil engineering technology, or a related field
- Experience with digital field-reporting platforms (e.g., Metafield/AgileFrameworks, FieldSync)
- OSHA 10- or 30-hour construction safety certification

## What We Offer

- Paid time off and paid holidays
